## The Importance of Total Darkness due to Light Sensitivity

Light is the primary regulator of the human circadian rhythm. Exposure to streetlights, electronics, or even early morning sun can signal the brain to wake up prematurely. For those suffering from insomnia, even the faintest glimmer of light can prevent the onset of sleep or interrupt the REM cycle.

## Key Features to Look For

When searching for the best sleep masks for insomnia and light sensitivity, several factors distinguish a superior product from a basic accessory:

*   **Contoured Design:** Flat masks can press against the eyelids, causing discomfort and interfering with Rapid Eye Movement (REM) sleep. 3D contoured cups allow the eyes to blink freely.
*   **Adjustability:** A sleep mask must fit securely without being too tight. Adjustable straps prevent the mask from slipping off during the night or causing headaches from excess pressure.
*   **Material Breathability:** Overheating can disrupt sleep just as much as light. Materials like memory foam or breathable fabrics ensure the skin remains cool.
*   **Zero Eye Pressure:** To maintain comfort throughout the night, the mask should rest on the cheekbones and forehead rather than the eyes themselves.
