# How Heavy Should Your Weighted Blanket Really Be?

Determining **how heavy should your weighted blanket really be** is the foundational step toward unlocking the therapeutic benefits of deep pressure stimulation. A blanket that is too light may fail to provide the desired calming effect, while one that is too heavy can feel restrictive or uncomfortable. Finding the ideal weight is essential for improving sleep quality and reducing anxiety naturally.

## The General Rule of Thumb

The most widely accepted guideline for selecting a weighted blanket is the "10 percent rule." Medical professionals and sleep experts generally recommend choosing a blanket that is approximately 10 percent of your total body weight. For many users, adding one or two extra pounds to this calculation provides the optimal level of comfort.

For example, an individual weighing 150 pounds would typically benefit most from a 15-pound blanket. However, personal preference plays a significant role. Some users prefer a heavier sensation for maximum grounding, while others may prefer a slightly lighter touch. ## Why Weight Matters

The effectiveness of these blankets relies on deep pressure therapy (DPT). This therapy stimulates the production of serotonin (the relaxation hormone) and melatonin (the sleep hormone) while reducing cortisol (the stress hormone). If you are constantly asking, "**How heavy should your weighted blanket really be?**" it is likely because you want to ensure these hormonal shifts occur effectively. Using the correct weight simulates the feeling of a warm hug, which helps calm the nervous system.

## Considerations for Different Users

While the 10 percent rule is a great starting point, different demographics have unique requirements.

### Adults
For most adults, blankets typically range between 12 and 25 pounds. If an individual falls in between standard weights (e.g., 130 lbs), it is usually recommended to round up to the next available weight class (e.g., a 15 lb blanket) rather than rounding down, as the weight is distributed across the entire surface area of the blanket.

### Children
Safety is paramount when selecting a blanket for a child. A weighted blanket should never be used for infants or toddlers who cannot move the blanket off themselves freely. For older children, the calculation of 10 percent of body weight plus one or two pounds is standard. Always prioritize safety and ensure the child can remove the blanket without assistance.

### Older Adults
For seniors, mobility issues should be considered. If an individual has joint pain or frailty, a lighter option—perhaps around 5 to 7 percent of body weight—might be more appropriate to provide comfort without causing strain.

## Choosing for Couples

When couples share a bed, the question of "**how heavy should your weighted blanket really be?**" becomes slightly more complex. If both partners are of similar size, a blanket weighing 10 percent of their combined weight is appropriate. However, if there is a significant weight difference, it is often more beneficial to use two separate blankets tailored to each person's specific needs. ## Size vs. Weight

It is important to note that a weighted blanket is designed to cover the person, not the entire bed. Unlike a comforter, it should not hang over the sides of the mattress. If the blanket hangs over the edge, gravity will pull it off the bed during the night. Therefore, the weight should be concentrated directly on the body for maximum therapeutic impact.
