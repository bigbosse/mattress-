# How to Choose a Weighted Blanket for Couples

Learning **how to choose a weighted blanket for couples** can transform restless nights into a shared experience of deep relaxation and improved sleep quality. While the benefits of deep pressure stimulation are well-documented for individuals, selecting a single blanket for two people requires careful consideration of weight, size, and material compatibility. This guide outlines the essential factors to ensure both partners achieve optimal comfort.

## Determining the Correct Weight

The most critical aspect of selecting a weighted blanket is finding the appropriate heaviness. The general rule for individuals is 10% of their body weight, but the math changes when two people share the covering.

When calculating the ideal weight for a couple, aim for approximately 7.5% of the combined body weight. For example, if the combined weight of the couple is 350 pounds, a blanket weighing roughly 25 to 30 pounds is often appropriate. However, if there is a significant weight difference between partners (e.g., more than a 50% variance), it may be more beneficial to purchase two individual twin-sized blankets rather than one large one. This ensures neither partner feels overwhelmed or under-stimulated by the pressure.

## Selecting the Right Size

Unlike standard comforters, a weighted blanket should not hang significantly over the edges of the bed. If the blanket is too wide, gravity will pull it toward the floor, causing it to slide off during the night.

For couples sharing a Queen or King-sized bed, look for a blanket specifically designed to fit the dimensions of the mattress top. ## Material and Temperature Regulation

Couples often have different thermal preferences; one may sleep hot while the other sleeps cold. To accommodate both, prioritizing breathability is essential. A weighted blanket with a cotton or bamboo cover offers better airflow than synthetic polyester or mink fabrics.

For those who fluctuate in temperature, look for blankets with removable covers. This allow users to switch between a warm, fleece duvet for winter and a cooling, moisture-wicking cover for summer, making the blanket useful year-round.

## Quality Construction and Filling

To ensure the blanket remains effective for two people, the internal construction must be durable. High-quality blankets utilize glass beads rather than plastic pellets, as glass is denser and creates a smoother profile. Furthermore, the stitching pattern matters. Small quilted squares or pockets prevent the beads from shifting to one side or pooling at the bottom.

Investing in a well-constructed **[weighted blanket](https://athometouch.com/weighted-blanket)** ensures that the filling remains evenly distributed, providing consistent therapy for both sleepers night after night. Proper weight distribution prevents the "fight for the blanket" scenario and ensures the deep pressure remains balanced across the entire bed.

## Final Thoughts

Understanding **how to choose a weighted blanket for couples** involves balancing the physical needs and sleep habits of two individuals. By carefully calculating the combined weight, selecting breathable materials, and ensuring high-quality stitching, couples can enjoy a restful, synchronized sleep environment.
