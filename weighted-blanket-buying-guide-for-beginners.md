# Weighted Blanket Buying Guide for Beginners

If restless nights have become the norm, this comprehensive **Weighted Blanket Buying Guide for Beginners** serves as the ultimate resource for finding deep, restorative relaxation. ## Understanding Deep Pressure Therapy

Before making a purchase, it is helpful to understand the mechanism behind the product. Weighted blankets utilize a therapeutic technique known as Deep Pressure Stimulation (DPS). The heavy weight mimics the sensation of being held or hugged. This pressure signals the body to reduce the production of cortisol (the stress hormone) while increasing serotonin and melatonin, the hormones responsible for relaxation and sleep.

## Choosing the Correct Weight

The most critical aspect of any **Weighted Blanket Buying Guide for Beginners** is determining the appropriate heaviness. A blanket that is too light will not provide the necessary therapeutic pressure, while one that is too heavy may feel restrictive or uncomfortable.

The general rule of thumb is to select a blanket that is approximately **10% of the user's body weight**, plus or minus one or two pounds.

*   **130 to 150 lbs:** A 15-pound blanket is usually ideal.
*   **150 to 200 lbs:** A 20-pound blanket provides better pressure.
*   **200+ lbs:** A 25-pound blanket or heavier is recommended.

When purchasing for children or older adults, it is advisable to consult a healthcare professional to ensure the weight is safe for their specific physical needs.

## Selecting the Right Fill and Fabric

The interior fill and exterior fabric determine the blanket's breathability, texture, and durability.

### Fill Materials
*   **Micro Glass Beads:** These are superior for weight distribution. They are smaller than grains of sand, allowing the blanket to lay smooth and flat against the body. They are also quieter and hold less heat than plastic options.
*   **Plastic Pellets:** Often found in budget-friendly options, these are larger and can create a "bean bag" texture. They tend to retain more body heat.

### Fabric Covers
*   **Cotton:** Ideal for hot sleepers due to its breathability and moisture-wicking properties.
*   **Minky/Fleece:** Best for cold climates or users who prefer a plush, warm texture.
*   **Bamboo:** A natural cooling fabric that is excellent for temperature regulation.

## Sizing Considerations

Unlike a traditional comforter, a weighted blanket should not hang over the edges of the bed. If the blanket hangs over the side, gravity may pull it off the bed entirely during the night. Therefore, the dimensions should align with the user's body size rather than the mattress size (King, Queen, or Twin). The goal is to cover the user snugly to maximize the deep pressure therapy effects.
