# How to Choose a Weighted Blanket for Side Sleepers

Understanding **how to choose a weighted blanket for side sleepers** can be the specific adjustment needed to transform a restless night into deep, restorative slumber. While these therapeutic covers are popular for anxiety relief and improved sleep quality, side sleeping creates unique pressure points that require careful consideration regarding weight, size, and material.

## Calculating the Ideal Weight

The general industry standard suggests selecting a blanket that is approximately 10% of the user's body weight. However, knowing **how to choose a weighted blanket for side sleepers** often involves slight adjustments to this rule.

Because side sleepers bear the weight on a narrower frame, the full 10% might feel restrictive or heavy on the hips. Many experts recommend that side sleepers opt for a blanket that is closer to 7% or 8% of their body weight, or simply round down if between standard weights. This lighter approach maintains the benefits of serotonin production and stress reduction while preventing morning stiffness.

## Sizing for the Person, Not the Bed

A common error when purchasing weighted bedding is selecting a size based on the mattress dimensions (Queen, King, etc.). For side sleepers, a blanket that is too large will hang over the edge of the bed. As the sleeper shifts positions, gravity will pull the heavy fabric toward the floor, eventually dragging the cover off the sleeper entirely.

The most effective option is a blanket sized to fit the individual's body. It should cover the user from the neck to the feet but should not drape significantly over the sides of the mattress. This ensures the therapeutic pressure remains constant throughout the night.

## Thermal Regulation and Materials

Side sleepers often curl up, which creates a pocket of trapped body heat. Consequently, breathability becomes a vital factor. Blankets filled with glass beads are generally preferred over plastic pellets, as glass is denser and takes up less space, allowing for better airflow.

Furthermore, the exterior fabric plays a massive role in temperature control. Natural fibers like bamboo or cotton are superior for preventing overheating compared to synthetic fleece or minky fabrics. Selecting the proper sleep aid requires analyzing sleep position dynamics.
